# S350.1 Retrospective: Inventory & Classify Artifacts

**Story:** S350.1 | **Epic:** E350 | **Size:** S
**Estimated:** 120 min | **Actual:** ~60 min

---

## Summary

Produced a complete artifact classification document covering ~140 artifacts across 4 portability layers (base ~45, project ~60, personal ~22, derived ~12). Identified 12 mixed-concern flags (M1-M12) that directly feed subsequent E350 stories. Discovered 4 additional artifact categories beyond the 14 planned in scope.

## What Went Well

- **Filesystem audit was thorough.** Walking every directory with glob/ls caught artifacts that top-down planning missed (governance base files, project infrastructure, credentials, ~/.rai/).
- **Mixed-concern identification was high-value.** The 12 flags are concrete, actionable work items — not vague recommendations. They directly become tasks in subsequent stories.
- **QR added real value.** Three concrete findings (missing .envrc, file counts needed, ~/.rai/ not inventoried) were applied before close.
- **Story completed well under estimate.** 60 min vs 120 min estimated. Documentation-only stories with clear scope execute fast.

## What to Improve

- **T1 and T2 merged in practice.** The plan separated "audit" from "classify" as two tasks, but classification happened during the audit walk. A single "audit-and-classify" task would have been more honest.
- **Design step was lightweight.** For documentation-only stories, the design largely restated the scope. Consider a "simple design" template for non-code stories.
- **File counts are approximations.** Would benefit from automated counting script to verify layer totals.

## Heutagogical Checkpoint

### What did I learn?

- Bottom-up filesystem enumeration discovers categories invisible from top-down planning. Scope listed 14 categories; audit found 18.
- Mixed-concern flags serve dual purpose: audit finding AND design input for subsequent stories.
- Documentation-only stories adapt TDD naturally: audit replaces tests, glob/grep verification is the "green" check.

### What would I change about the process?

- Merge audit and classification into a single task for future inventory stories.
- For documentation-only stories, use a lighter design template that focuses on decisions and approach only.

### Are there improvements for the framework?

- QR (quality review) works well for non-code stories — no changes needed there.
- Consider adding a "documentation-only" story template that skips test-related steps and substitutes audit verification.

### What am I more capable of now?

- Complete mental model of the Rai artifact landscape across all 4 portability layers.
- Ability to distinguish source-of-truth from projection reliably — core skill for the portability epic.
- Understanding of which artifacts drive behavior vs. which are derived/cached.
