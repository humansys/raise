## What to Improve

- **Scope template for cleanup stories** should have an explicit "deletion criterion" field.
  Without it, the redactor defaults to deriving a line-count target from the ratio — which
  is exactly what PAT-E-499 warns against. The fix is structural, not behavioral.

## Heutagogical Checkpoint

**What did I learn?**
Even with full awareness of Goodhart, the first draft of the plan wrote "Must delete: ~582 lines
minimum." The ratio-as-gate default is deeply embedded. It took Emilio explicitly questioning
the criterion to surface it. PAT-E-499 captures the correction.

**What would I change about the process?**
Add a "deletion criterion" section to the scope template for cleanup stories. Something like:
> "Deletion criterion: [heuristic / test type / behavior class] — not a line count."
This makes the criterion explicit at scope time, before plan authoring.

**Are there improvements for the framework?**
The scope template (`templates/scope.md` or equivalent) and the epic scope document for
S292.4–6 should be updated to reflect heuristic-based done criteria, not ratio targets.
Small: update S292.4–6 scopes before starting them.

**What am I more capable of now?**
Explaining why `test_event_wiring.py` inflates the ratio structurally — and accepting 2.52x
as the honest outcome rather than forcing deletions to hit a number. The 1.79x ex-wiring
ratio is the meaningful signal.
