## What Went Well

- **Blueprint-first approach worked** — having `raise-blueprint.md` before reading Anthropic articles transformed the research from "what they say" to "how we compare." The 4-dimension scoring grid gave objective structure to subjective tradeoffs.
- **G10 tension resolved cleanly** — the structured deliberation (state positions, gather evidence, test against principles, decide) produced a principled and defensible position. No hand-waving.
- **Enterprise implications fell out naturally** — the 4 divergences mapped directly to enterprise features without forcing. This produced `enterprise-design-implications.md` as a genuine insight, not just output.
- **Evidence triangulation caught weak claims** — Art.1, Art.2, Art.4 are mostly assertion-based. Noting this explicitly prevented over-weighting Anthropic's authority.
- **RAISE-806 (E-PATTERNS) created as structured output** — research immediately translated into actionable backlog items with proper fixVersions and links.

## What to Improve

- **Subagent hang on implementation phase** — the forked subagent for T1-T5 (article reading via WebFetch) hung without output. Research stories with external web reads should NOT fork implementation to subagents. Add note to `/rai-story-run` skill for `type: analysis`.
- **Session interruptions** — two context resets occurred. Post-compaction recovery worked (journal + git log) but cost ~20 min. The `rai-progress.md` pattern we identified as a gap (G4) would have helped here — ironic that we suffered the very problem we researched.
- **OSS/Enterprise framing came late** — identified after the benchmark matrix, not before. Should be in evaluation criteria from story start.

## Heutagogical Checkpoint

1. **What did I learn?** Benchmarking external patterns requires a baseline of your own system first. Without RaiSE's blueprint, the research would have been about Anthropic, not about RaiSE. Also learned: Anthropic's evidence quality varies significantly by article (Art.6 has data; Art.1/Art.4 are mostly assertions).

2. **What would I change?** Put OSS vs Enterprise as a 5th evaluation dimension from the start. Include it in the epic scope scoring criteria, not as a post-analysis insight.

3. **Framework improvements?** Three applied:
   - PAT-E-004: Baseline-first research methodology
   - PAT-E-005: OSS/Enterprise segmentation in evaluation criteria
   - PAT-E-006: Analysis stories keep WebFetch in main thread, not subagents

4. **What am I more capable of?** Evaluating external agent patterns with RaiSE's lens — I now know which principles are load-bearing (P1, P2, P5) vs. which are refinable (P7 ceremony scaling). This makes future research stories faster.
