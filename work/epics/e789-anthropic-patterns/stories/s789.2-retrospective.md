## Heutagogical Checkpoint

### 1. What did I learn?

G13 was the most surprising finding. The epic scope described it as "contract negotiation between evaluator and generator" — which sounds like agent autonomy beyond P1. But reading the actual articles, Anthropic doesn't do scope negotiation between agents. They do:
- Unilateral scope expansion by an initializer (≈ our story-design)
- Quality assessment by an evaluator (≈ our quality-review)
- Resource calibration by a lead agent (≈ our story-plan)

All of these are execution-level activities within human-defined goals. The gap was mischaracterized — and recognizing that led to ADR-034, which is more valuable than a simple "Reject G13."

### 2. What would I change about the process?

Include URL verification as part of `/rai-epic-design` — when source articles are listed, verify URLs are accessible before committing the scope. A 404 mid-research wastes time.

### 3. Are there improvements for the framework?

Yes — ADR-034 (P1 reformulation) is a framework-level improvement. The simplicity test pattern (PAT-new-3) should be formally added to the evaluation methodology for all future research stories.

### 4. What am I more capable of now?

Two things:
- Distinguishing scope-level decisions (human-owned) from execution-level negotiation (agent-compatible). This distinction will inform every future P1-related analysis.
- The simplicity test ("would a developer skip this?") as an instant filter. Applied it to G2 and got a clear OSS vs Enterprise line in seconds.
