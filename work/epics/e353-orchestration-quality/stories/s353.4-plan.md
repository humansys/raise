## Execution Order

```
T1 (gather evidence) → T2 (write report) → T3 (review)
```

**Rationale:** Strictly sequential. Evidence must be verified (T1) before the report can reference it with confidence (T2). Report must exist before it can be reviewed (T3). No parallelism possible -- each task depends on the previous.

Risk-first ordering is natural here: T1 is the riskiest task (if evidence doesn't match the design's claims, the methodology or parity scores need revision). Better to discover that early.

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|:----------:|:------:|------------|
| Evidence discrepancy (design claims don't match actual artifacts) | Low | Medium | T1 catches this early; adjust parity scores if needed |
| Single-story sample weakness challenged in review | Medium | Low | Already documented as caveat in design; acknowledge honestly |
| Weighted parity calculation disputed | Low | Medium | Show methodology transparently; weights come from design doc |
