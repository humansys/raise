## Academic Papers & Surveys (Very High Evidence)

### 1. The Prompt Report: A Systematic Survey

**Source**: [The Prompt Report: A Systematic Survey of Prompt Engineering Techniques](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.06608)
- **Type**: Primary (Academic Survey)
- **Evidence Level**: Very High
- **Key Finding**: Establishes taxonomy of 58 LLM prompting techniques with 33 standardized terms; updated February 2025
- **Relevance**: Provides comprehensive vocabulary and classification for structured prompt design
- **Citation Context**: arXiv preprint, comprehensive academic survey

### 2. Systematic Survey of Prompt Engineering in LLMs

**Source**: [A Systematic Survey of Prompt Engineering in Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.07927)
- **Type**: Primary (Academic Survey)
- **Evidence Level**: Very High
- **Key Finding**: Five prompting strategies vary in complexity/contextualization: Zero-Shot, Few-Shot, Chain-of-Thought, Role-Based, ISO 21502-Guided
- **Relevance**: Hierarchical framework for selecting prompt complexity based on task requirements
- **Citation Context**: arXiv preprint, February 2024

### 3. Hybrid Framework for AI-Augmented Systematic Reviews

**Source**: [A hybrid framework for creating AI-augmented systematic literature reviews](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11301-025-00522-8)
- **Type**: Primary (Academic Research)
- **Evidence Level**: Very High
- **Key Finding**: Framework anchored in transparency, validity, reliability, comprehensiveness, and reflective agency; employs iterative prompt engineering with robust human oversight
- **Relevance**: Epistemological principles for AI research workflows
- **Citation Context**: Management Review Quarterly, 2025

### 4. Evidence Triangulator Using LLMs

**Source**: [Evidence triangulator: using large language models to extract and synthesize causal evidence](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-62783-x)
- **Type**: Primary (Academic Research)
- **Evidence Level**: Very High
- **Key Finding**: Two-step extraction (concepts first, then relations) outperforms one-step; F1=0.86 for effect direction, F1=0.96 for statistical significance
- **Relevance**: Demonstrates structured prompt patterns for systematic evidence extraction
- **Citation Context**: Nature Communications, 2025

### 5. Prompt Engineering for Statistical Reasoning
